DIY Outdoor Gear Maintenance That Actually Extends Lifespan

Good gear maintenance is mostly boring, consistent habits rather than occasional deep-cleans. Here's what actually moves the needle.

1. Dry gear fully before storage — moisture trapped in packed-away gear is the single biggest cause of premature breakdown (mildew, zipper corrosion, fabric degradation). 2. Re-waterproof seasonally, not just when it visibly fails — by the time water is visibly soaking through, the coating has been degraded for a while already. 3. Store outside direct sunlight — UV exposure breaks down synthetic fabrics and elastic even when gear isn't in use.

Expensive specialty cleaning products rarely outperform basic, gear-appropriate care done consistently. Consistency beats intensity here.
